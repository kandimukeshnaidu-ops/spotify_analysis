Key Insights

  • The popularity distribution is heavily right-skewed, indicating that most songs on Spotify have low popularity while only a small fraction achieve high popularity.
  • Danceability varies significantly across genres, with reggaeton and disco showing the highest median danceability, while punk-rock and acoustic genres tend to be less danceable.
  • Popularity is moderately positively correlated with energy and loudness, suggesting that energetic and louder tracks are more likely to perform well.
  • Instrumentalness shows a negative correlation with popularity, indicating that vocal tracks generally attract more listeners.
  • Popular songs tend to be more energetic, louder, and less instrumental compared to unpopular songs, though no single feature fully determines popularity.

Limitations

  • The dataset does not contain release year information, preventing temporal trend analysis.
  • Popularity is influenced by external factors such as marketing, artist fame, and platform algorithms, which are not captured in the dataset.
  • Correlation analysis does not imply causation and only indicates associations between features.
